Manage My Surgery TM (MMS) is a secure and HIPAA compliant platform created by doctors to support patients and their caregivers throughout their entire surgical journey, from planning to recovery and rehabilitation.
Our Vision is to empower patients to help better manage their surgeries by offering them an easy-to-use, evidence-based management tool that serves as a virtual navigator.
MMS also provides doctors and the care team a platform to monitor and enhance patient engagement.

Our Story
Higgs Boson Health designs and implements products to improve healthcare delivery through information technology. Higgs Boson Health consists of a team of seasoned physicians and information technology professionals who believe that the right information, in the right hands, at the right time can improve patient satisfaction and deliver better outcomes.
Our Team
Nandan Lad, MD, PhD
Nandan is a Stanford-trained neurosurgeon, scientist, and entrepreneur with extensive clinical, research and medical device experience. He is passionate about innovation and entrepreneurship and believes it is essential to combine world-class clinical care with high-quality research and information technology to improve patient lives and transform our standard of care. He currently is an Associate Professor of Neurosurgery at Duke University Medical Center and Director of the Duke NeuroInnovations Program.
Rajeev Dharmapurikar
Rajeev is an accomplished executive with an extensive entrepreneurial and enterprise background. As the cofounder of four start-ups, he has demonstrated his acumen as a strategic thinker, effective decision maker and pragmatic businessman. He has created and delivered complex technology platforms while building, mentoring and scaling high performance teams.
Ziad Gellad, MD, MPH
Ziad is a Johns Hopkins and Duke-trained gastroenterologist with a passion for improving healthcare quality and value. He is a health services researcher, physician executive and entrepreneur. In each role, the patient and the patient experience has been central. He is an Associate Professor of Medicine at Duke where he also serves as Associate Vice Chair for Clinical Innovation in the Department of Medicine and Director of Quality for the Division of Gastroenterology.
Joe Knight, PhD
Joe is an Army Veteran and medical device entrepreneur with nearly 15 years of experience in medical device innovation and commercialization. His passion is in combining the disciplines of engineering, medicine and business to solve the most pressing needs in healthcare, ensuring that innovative technologies get to the patients and physicians that can benefit most from them. He holds a PhD in Biomedical Engineering and is a graduate of Stanford’s Biodesign Innovation program.
